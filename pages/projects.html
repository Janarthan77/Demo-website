Full 3D Pipeline

VR Headset Launch

Challenge

The client needed a photoreal 60-second CGI teaser that could go viral across social platforms within a tight 4-week window. With no physical product prototype available for scanning, the entire headset had to be reconstructed from engineering CAD files — a process that demanded meticulous polygon cleanup, UV unwrapping, and PBR material authoring from scratch.

Solution

The headset was modelled in Cinema 4D using imported CAD geometry, then handed off to Redshift for GPU-accelerated rendering. A 12-light HDR rig was built to emphasise the interplay between the glossy visor and matte-textured housing. Dynamic camera sweeps were choreographed using C4D's Camera Morph tag, with depth-of-field pushed to cinematic extremes to create a sense of scale and premium quality.

Impact

The teaser exceeded every KPI the client had projected. Within the first 24 hours of going live, it accumulated 2.5 million views across Instagram Reels, TikTok, and YouTube Shorts, generating a groundswell of pre-launch hype that contributed directly to a sold-out first production run. The campaign was subsequently shortlisted for a Promax Digital award.

  • Role: Lead 3D Artist & Motion Designer
  • Timeline: 4 Weeks
  • Outcome: 2.5M Views across social within 24 hours.

VFX Compositing

Neon Genesis Web Series

Challenge

Working on an indie production means zero budget buffer and no second chances. The six-episode sci-fi series required over 150 VFX shots delivered at broadcast quality — yet the production had been shot on a consumer-grade mirrorless camera under variable lighting conditions on locations that had none of the greenscreen infrastructure a traditional shoot would use.

Solution

Every shot was triaged in Nuke by complexity: wire and rig removals were batched using automatic inpainting scripts; rotoscoping was handled with a combination of Silhouette and Nuke's built-in roto tools; and the dystopian city extensions were built in Houdini as fully procedural environments before being composited through multi-layer EXR renders. Holographic screen replacements used custom GLSL shaders to simulate scanlines and chromatic aberration realistically.

Impact

The series launched on YouTube and Vimeo to immediate critical attention, accumulating 800K views across the six episodes within the first month. It earned a nomination for Best VFX at IndieWebFest and was cited by Screen Daily as "a benchmark for what independent productions can achieve with creative pipeline engineering."

  • Role: Compositing Supervisor
  • Timeline: 8 Weeks
  • Outcome: Nominated for Best VFX at IndieWebFest.

Motion Graphics

Spotify Wrapped Campaign

Challenge

The Spotify Wrapped moment is one of the most anticipated social media events of the year, and the brief demanded a visual identity that felt fresh, shareable, and unmistakably Spotify — all within a brutal 2-week turnaround. The animation needed to work equally well as a 15-second Instagram Story, a 30-second Reel, and a full 60-second YouTube pre-roll with no layout re-work.

Solution

A modular motion system was designed in After Effects, with each text block and colour field treated as an independently animatable component driven by audio-reactive expressions. The kinetic typography was rigged using Motion Bro expressions so that any editorial change to the script would auto-reflow the animation. Camera transitions were timed to the downbeats of the custom track using Marker-to-Keyframe scripts, ensuring perfect audio-visual synchrony across all three format variants.

Impact

The campaign drove a 15% higher click-through rate compared to the previous year's Wrapped creative, with a 22% uplift in Story completion rate. The modular system was so well-received internally that Spotify's in-house team requested a handover of the After Effects template for ongoing use in regional market adaptations across 12 countries.

  • Role: Motion Graphics Animator
  • Timeline: 2 Weeks
  • Outcome: 15% higher click-through-rate than previous campaigns.

Motion Graphics / Commercial

Nike Air Max Global Campaign

Challenge

Nike required a 30-second global commercial for the Air Max 360 launch that could communicate the shoe's signature Air cushioning technology in a way that felt visceral and cinematic — not clinical. The brief explicitly ruled out product-on-white footage and pushed instead for a fully animated world where physics became the hero, all under a punishing 3-week delivery schedule.

Solution

A proprietary physics-based animation system was developed in Cinema 4D using Houdini for fluid and soft-body simulations to visualise the air compression beneath each footfall in real time. The shoe itself was rendered in Redshift with sub-surface scattering applied to the translucent Air unit. Every animation beat was locked to a custom trap music track composed specifically for the campaign, with keyframes driven by audio amplitude data extracted via AEJuice's Audio2Keyframes tool.

Impact

The commercial amassed 50M+ cross-platform impressions in the first week of release across YouTube, Instagram, and Nike's owned digital channels. It was featured in Adweek's "Best Commercials of the Quarter" roundup and credited by Nike's marketing team as a significant driver of the Air Max 360's sell-through exceeding projections by 30% in the launch quarter.

  • Role: Lead Motion Designer
  • Timeline: 3 Weeks
  • Outcome: 50M+ cross-platform impressions, featured in Adweek.
