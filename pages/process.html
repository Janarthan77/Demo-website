The 3-Step Pipeline

How we move from initial contact to final delivery — no surprises, no ambiguity.

01

Discovery & Quote

You submit an inquiry through the contact form detailing the project scope, desired mood, and any reference material. We then hop on a focused 15-minute discovery call to align on creative vision, technical constraints, deadlines, and budget. Following the call, I conduct an internal feasibility review and assemble a detailed Statement of Work (SOW) outlining deliverables, milestones, and terms. A formal quote is issued within 24 hours — no guesswork, no hidden costs.

02

Asset Handoff & Edit

Once the deposit is received and the SOW is signed, we kick off the production phase. You provide footage, brand guidelines, music direction, and any raw assets via our shared Frame.io workspace or Google Drive folder — both are set up for you on day one. I begin with a structured offline edit or motion blockout, establishing the timing, pacing, and overall creative skeleton before committing to heavy rendering or compositing. Regular progress check-ins via Slack keep you informed at every milestone without disrupting the creative flow.

03

Review & Final Delivery

Version 1 is shared via a Frame.io review link, enabling you to drop precise frame-accurate comments directly on the timeline — no confusing email chains. We iterate through the agreed revision rounds within the defined scope, refining colour, pacing, and sound until the piece is locked. Final masters are exported in all requested codecs and resolutions (ProRes 4444, H.264, HEVC, DNxHD, or platform-specific presets) and delivered via a secure Google Drive handoff folder alongside all associated project documentation.

Clear Revision Policies

Transparent rules protect your budget and keep the project on schedule for everyone involved.

Revisions vs. New Direction

What counts as a revision?

A revision is any change that refines the approved creative direction — colour grade tweaks, timing adjustments, copy swaps, or minor animation polish. A new creative direction — changing the concept, visual style, music genre, or requesting a structural re-edit after V1 approval — constitutes a scope change and is billed separately at the standard hourly rate of $150/hr.

Included Rounds

Standard revision rounds

All project quotes explicitly include a set number of revision rounds (typically 2–3, as listed in the turnaround table). Each round is a single batch of consolidated feedback submitted within 48 hours of receiving the review link. Unconsolidated feedback submitted piecemeal across multiple emails or messages counts as separate rounds.

Rush Delivery

Need it faster?

Rush delivery is available on a case-by-case basis depending on current studio capacity. Deliverables required within 48 hours of project kick-off carry a +50% rush surcharge on the total project fee. Rush requests must be declared at inquiry stage — retrofitting rush requirements mid-project may not always be feasible.

Source File Licensing

Do you need the project files?

Final rendered deliverables are included in every project quote. Source files (After Effects, Premiere Pro, Cinema 4D, Nuke, DaVinci projects) are available under a separate Source File License for an additional buyout fee — typically 30–50% of the original project cost. Full IP transfer and white-label rights can also be negotiated for agency partnerships.

What to Prepare Before Kick-Off

The smoother your asset handoff, the faster your project moves through the pipeline. Here's exactly what I need from you on day one.

  • Brand Guidelines Logos in vector format (AI / SVG / EPS), approved typefaces, primary and secondary colour palettes with HEX/CMYK values, and any existing brand animation standards or motion manifesto.
  • Reference Videos A curated mood board or 3–5 reference videos that capture the tone, pacing, and visual language you're aiming for. Vimeo/YouTube links are fine — the more specific, the better.
  • Raw Footage All camera originals in their native codec (BRAW, R3D, ProRes, Log-C, S-Log) organised by shoot day and scene. Avoid converting to H.264 before delivery — working from camera originals ensures maximum quality headroom.
  • Music & SFX Direction Licensed music tracks or a clear brief on the desired genre, tempo, and emotional register. If you're using licensed music from Artlist, Epidemic Sound, or similar, please share the licensed files directly — not streaming links.
  • Hard Deadlines The final air date, broadcast slot, or campaign launch date — plus any intermediate milestones (e.g. client presentation, internal stakeholder review). This drives the production schedule and determines whether rush fees apply.
  • Budget Range A realistic budget envelope helps me tailor the scope, recommend the right production tier, and avoid proposals that miss the mark. There is no minimum — but knowing the range upfront saves time for both of us.
